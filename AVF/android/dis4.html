    What is the average cost and bandwidth limits per user (in general) in each of the three markets? How may this impact development?

    In the UK, the number of plans you can choose from seems unlimited compared to the US and seems to be very specialized per the type of phone. Looking at one of the most popular carriers is Vodafone the plans range from £10 all the way to £62. What seems to be very different is that the plans only come with 1 GB of data included. Each additional 50MB is £1which can get very expensive very quickly. The next GB will cost close to £20.

    The US has similar costs across carriers. They typically have costs for data within tiers that start as low as 300MBs and typically go up to 5GBs. The tiers for bandwidth range from $20 - $50. Verizon and AT&T follow these tiers pretty closely. Sprint, being another large carrier, is known for it’s unlimited data plans. While it is unlimited, it is still the cost of the highest plans on Verizon and AT&T and at those high levels, the majority of users don’t go over their limits.

    In Japan, data plans seem to be mostly unlimited across the board. There was debate by many carriers to begin placing caps on data usage but the possibility of losing consumers has deterred to officially put in place those caps. One of the most popular carriers, au, charges for phone, text and unlimited data starting at ~$83. For the unlimited data portion the user is paying ~$67. When comparing to a plan in the U.S. the numbers seem very comparable.

    When thinking about developing, it seems that the data limits in Japan and the US are more forgiving and you can really pack a large amount of data into applications. In the UK, you have to be very careful as to how much data your users are using as it can become very expensive very quick.
